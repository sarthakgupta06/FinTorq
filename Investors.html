Investors

  • Reatil Investors - Investors who buy and sell securities for their personal accounts. They typically invest smaller amounts of money compared to institutional investors and often rely on brokerage services to access financial markets.


  • DIIs - Domestic Institutional Investors are financial institutions within a country that invest in the local financial markets using funds from domestic sources. They include mutual funds, insurance companies, banks, pension funds, and other entities that manage funds on behalf of individual and institutional clients.


  • AMCs - Asset Management Companies are financial firms managing investment funds for clients, offering products like mutual funds and ETFs. Examples include BlackRock, Vanguard, and Fidelity.


  • FIIs - Foreign Institutional Investors are overseas entities, such as hedge funds, pension funds, and mutual funds, that invest in the financial markets of another country. FIIs bring significant capital and contribute to the liquidity and stability of the host country's financial markets.
