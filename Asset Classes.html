Asset Classes

  • Fixed Income Instruments - Fixed income instruments are financial assets that provide investors with a predictable and steady stream of income over time. They include bonds, certificates of deposit (CDs), and other debt securities issued by governments, corporations, or financial institutions.


  • Equity - Equity represents ownership in a company through shares of common stock, providing shareholders with ownership rights and potential dividends. It reflects the residual interest in a company's assets after settling liabilities.


  • Real Estate - Real estate refers to physical property, including land, buildings, and natural resources, that can be bought, sold, or leased for residential, commercial, or industrial purposes.


  • Commodities - Commodities are raw materials or primary agricultural products that can be traded in their standardized form on commodity exchanges. They include items like gold, crude oil, agricultural products and metals.
